Before it is a vessel, it is a spiral — a record of every turn it survived.
The Wheel

Ten seconds of stillness, held for an hour.

Centring is the hardest thing we teach. The wheel spins at three hundred revolutions a minute, and your hands must convince the clay it isn't moving at all. Every bowl in our store passed through this moment.

The Blade

What you remove matters more than what you add.

Trimming is subtraction as devotion. A sharpened rib against leather-hard clay, peeling away everything the form doesn't need — until the foot ring is light enough to sing when you flick it.

The Glaze

The kiln has the last word — in orange.

We mix our signature glaze from iron, ash, and patience. At 1,260 degrees it melts, runs, and settles into ambers and burnt oranges no brush could ever paint. You don't choose the final colour. You earn it.

The Shelf

No two pieces. Not once. Not ever.

Glaze runs where it wants to run. Flame licks where it wants to lick. Each firing is a negotiation with the kiln — which is why the piece you take home exists exactly once in the world.
